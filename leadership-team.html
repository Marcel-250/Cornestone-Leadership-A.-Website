Leadership Practice Team

The leadership practice team is responsible for developing and nurturing student leadership skills through various programs and activities. Through debates and other interactive sessions, students are encouraged to think critically, express themselves confidently, and develop strong leadership qualities.

Student Leadership Programs & Activities

Students' Morning Fellowships

Daily sessions to develop public speaking skills, moral guidance, and spiritual growth for students.


Debates

Structured debates to improve critical thinking, argumentation skills, and student confidence in public discussions.


Public Speaking Sessions

Workshops and practice sessions that mold students into confident speakers, preparing them for leadership roles.


Model United Nations (MUN)

Simulated UN sessions to train students in diplomacy, negotiation, and international relations.


Other Leadership Activities

Includes student mentorship, team-building exercises, community service projects, and leadership skill workshops.
